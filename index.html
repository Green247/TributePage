Biography

Don Williams was an American country singer and songwriter born on May 27, 1939, in Floydada, Texas. He grew up in Portland, Texas, and graduated from Gregory-Portland High School in 1958. Williams began his music career as a member of the folk-pop trio, The Pozo-Seco Singers, in the mid-1960s.

After the group disbanded in 1971, Williams moved to Nashville, Tennessee, and began his solo career. He signed with JMI Records and released his debut album, "Don Williams, Volume One," in 1973. The album featured the hit single "The Shelter of Your Eyes," which reached number 14 on the country charts.

Williams quickly became known for his smooth baritone voice and understated, laid-back style. He released a string of successful albums throughout the 1970s, including "Visions" (1977) and "Expressions" (1978), both of which reached number one on the country charts. During this time, Williams had a number of hit singles, including "Tulsa Time," "She Never Knew Me," and "It Must Be Love."

In the 1980s, Williams continued to enjoy success with hits such as "Love Me Over Again" and "That's the Thing About Love." He also began to collaborate with other artists, including Emmylou Harris and Waylon Jennings. Williams' popularity continued into the 1990s, with hits such as "Lord Have Mercy on a Country Boy" and "I Believe in You," which became his biggest hit, reaching number one on the country charts and crossing over to the pop charts. Throughout his career, Williams was known for his warm, friendly demeanor and his dedication to his family. He retired from music in 2016, citing health reasons, and passed away on September 8, 2017, at the age of 78. Williams was posthumously inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame in 2020. His legacy lives on through his music, which continues to be beloved by fans around the world.

Awards

Don Williams was a highly acclaimed American country music singer and songwriter. Throughout his career, he received numerous awards and recognitions for his contributions to the music industry. Here are some of the most notable ones:

Academy of Country Music Awards:

  • 1978 Single Record of the Year for "Tulsa Time"
  • 1980 Male Vocalist of the Year
  • 1981 Male Vocalist of the Year
  • 1981 Album of the Year for "I Believe in You"

Country Music Association Awards:

  • 1978 Album of the Year for "Expressions"
  • 1980 Male Vocalist of the Year
  • 1981 Male Vocalist of the Year

Grammy Awards:

  • 1978 Best Country Vocal Performance, Male for "Tulsa Time"
  • 1981 Best Country Vocal Performance, Male for "I Believe in You"

Country Music Hall of Fame:

  • Don Williams was inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame in 2010, in recognition of his contributions to the genre over a career that spanned more than four decades.

Other Awards:

  • American Music Award for Favorite Country Album in 1981 for "I Believe in You"
  • Academy of Country Music Pioneer Award in 1998
  • BMI Icon Award in 2008 for his contributions to the music industry as a songwriter
